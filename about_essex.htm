 The Essex Cypriot Society is here to help you. Here you will find information about Essex University and our small but beloved town, Colchester.

About Essex:

Colchester is located 60 miles northeast of London. Small, beautiful and cosy, Colchester is considered as one of the best universities cities in the UK. The big number of international students increases the culture diversity and everyone in Colchester feels like home. Colchester provides everything that a student wants and combines excellent culture and leisure.

1) Culture:

Museums: Colchester houses several museums. The Castle Museum, found within Colchester Castle, features an extensive exhibit on Roman Colchester. Nearby are Hollytrees Museum, a social history museum with children's exhibits in the former home of Charles Gray, and the town's Natural History Museum, located in the former All Saints' Church. Tymperley's Clock Museum, located in the town centre in a 15th century timber-framed house, once home to William Gilbert, now houses the Bernard Mason clock collection.

Arts: Opened in 1972, the Mercury Theatre is one the region's leading repertory theatres. Next door is Colchester Arts Centre,a multi-function arts venue located in the former St Mary-at-the-Walls church, and home of the Colchester Beer Festival. Headgate Theatre is also in Colchester.

Zoo: Colchester Zoo is a large zoo based on the outskirts of the town.

2) Shopping:

Tesco: For the daily-life shopping you can buy your food and groceries from Tesco which is only 15 minutes away from the Uni. Also in Tesco you can find almost everything you want: stationery, bath complementaries, and house accessories.

Argos: Argos it is the largest general-goods retailer in the UK. Here you can buy anything for Home and Garden, Kitchen and Laundry, Toys and Games, PC’s and Phones and so on.

American Shopping: And of course in the town centre you will find some of the world’s most popular branches for clothing shopping.

3) Nightlife:

Ok, now let’s get down to business.  Colchester has long been the capital of Essex for a good night out on the town. You can find several clubs, pubs and lounges that will definitely keep you entertained throughout your university years. The most famous are: Liquid and Envy, Yates, Missoula, Playhouse and Qube.
